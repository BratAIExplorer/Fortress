# 🔍 FORTRESS FACT-CHECK — The Simple Explanation

## What Is It? (Explain Like I'm 10)

Imagine you see someone on Instagram say:
> "This stock is AMAZING! It grows 30% every year!"

But you're not sure if they're telling the truth.

**Fortress Fact-Check does this:**
- You paste the stock name
- Fortress checks the REAL numbers (from official documents)
- It tells you: "Actually, it grew 28%, not 30%. Close, but a bit exaggerated."

That's it. **It's like a fact-checker for stock tips.**

---

## The 3 Things Fortress Fact-Check Checks

```
1. GROWTH (Does the company grow as fast as claimed?)
   Example: Influencer says "CAGR 30%" → Fortress checks actual numbers

2. VALUATION (Is it cheap or expensive?)
   Example: Influencer says "PE 15" → Fortress confirms it's a good price

3. SAFETY (Is the company risky or stable?)
   Example: Influencer doesn't mention debt → Fortress flags it as a risk
```

## How It Works Behind the Scenes

```
User enters: NESCO
    ↓
Fortress pulls data from:
  • Yahoo Finance (stock prices)
  • NSE/BSE filings (Indian company data)
  • Annual reports (revenue, profit, debt)
  • Company announcements (order books, deals)
    ↓
AI analyzes (using Claude):
  • Matches influencer claim to metric
  • Checks if numbers are accurate
  • Flags missing critical info
  • Scores overall credibility
    ↓
Result generated (3-5 seconds)
    ↓
Saved to database (trains AI for next time)
```
